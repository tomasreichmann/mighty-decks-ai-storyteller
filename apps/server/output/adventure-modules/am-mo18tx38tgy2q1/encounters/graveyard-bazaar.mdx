# Graveyard Bazaar

💡 This is a social/downtime encounter. There is no combat. Players can spend their resources to upgrade the ship, buy and sell items, work on projects, and interact with different factions to gain information or build relationships before a large concert event.

_Neon symbols flare against the stitched-together hulls, lighting the maze of wrecked ships that form the Graveyard Bazaar. <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="smugglers" />, scavengers, cultists, and wanderers drift between makeshift stalls and glowing stages, their shadows broken by the pulse of cheap generators and drifting lantern drones._

_Above, the electrified scrap-world crackles with blue lightning, illuminating the silent orbit of shattered cruisers and alien husks. Plasma welders spark in the dark as crews repair battered vessels, music vibrates the rusty floor on which you stand from nearby cargo stages, and traders shout over each other over intercom in half a dozen dialects._

_For one night, the graveyard lives — a neon carnival built atop a site of ancient battle. As you dock, a sense of safety washes over you. You finally release a breath that you didn't realize you were holding._

-   There are plenty of things to do here:

-   There will be a big concert later. You see people rushing to finish work so they can make it.

## Buy upgrades for your ship

-   Each improvement costs Resources times next level. (E.g. Upgrade lasers from level 1 to level 2 costs 2 resources).
-   📅 If you rescued the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="smugglers" /> ship in **Transport in Distress**: You meet the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="smugglers" /> you rescued earlier, giving you a 3 Resource discount on systems (either new or upgrades).
-   **There are no more free rooms on your ship**, but you can hire a crew to break into the sealed corridor, see: **Break into the sealed Corridor**. You can still upgrade existing systems.
-   Systems available to install:
    -   Med bay: Reduces Injuries by 1 + Effect. (1 Injury per person per level per round)
    -   Ritual room: +1 Effect on rituals.
    -   Teleport room: Can teleport crew in the room to a room in a nearby ship which does not have active shields.

## Buy and sell equipment

-   You can buy any basic weapon or armor, most are for 1 or two Resources. Modified goods are not available
-   You can sell most things for half the price.
-   **5 missiles** 1x Resources
-   **Instant sealant** (Insulating Resource) 1x Resources
-   **Synth-blood injectors** (Healing x2) 1x Resources
-   **Spin battery** (Pushing Surge) 1x Resources
-   **Imperial standard issue brandy** (Comfort x2) 1x Resources
-   **Imperial standard issue brandy** (Comfort x2) 1x Resources

## Work on your projects

You can spend downtime to learn a skill, a ritual, or work on a long-term project. You can also spend downtime to recover from stress and injuries.

## Ask around for help or advice

You can find experts to help you with your current projects or to teach you new skills in exchange for some Resources.

🔎 If you look for Void technology or information about the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-horror" />, you find a group of Void cultists that provide protection for the bazaar. They carry some kind of alien relic that should create an invisible protective bubble that prevents sub-space tearing. The cultists are very protective of their technology and will not let you near it, nor will they discuss it. If threatened, they teleport with the relic away.

### Concert

_As dusk settles over the scrap-world, the Graveyard Bazaar transforms. Stalls are being disassembled and the low hum of commerce is replaced by the thumping bass of music. Stages, fashioned from salvaged cargo containers, come alive with holographic projections and strobing lights. A cacophony of music fills the void—from the guttural chants of a death-cult choir to the high-energy synth-rock of augmented ex-soldiers, and the hypnotic, dissonant melodies of a <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-seer" />'s solo performance. Crowds of <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="smugglers" />, mechanics, and wanderers dance on makeshift floors, their faces lit by neon and the occasional pyrotechnic burst. The air is thick with the smell of cheap synth-ale, exotic foods, and the electric charge of a festival in full swing._

-   All your Distress is removed.

🎭 **You finally have time to unwind and reflect on your journey. What are you thinking about?**

### Party's over

_As the last band took their bow, the crowd slowly dispersed, leaving behind a scattering of empty bottles and a lingering scent of ozone and synth-ale. The docked ships are leaving one by one and the sense of unease returns. You decide to not dawdle and prepare to leave._

-   🔎 If you check on the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-seers" />, they are gone.
