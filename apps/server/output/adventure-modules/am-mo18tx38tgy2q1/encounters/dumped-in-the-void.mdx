# Dumped in the Void

💡 This is the introductory chapter of the campaign. The players are introduced to their characters and the ship they have been exiled on. They need to work together to get the ship operational and escape the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-horror" /> that appears as they are about to leave.

## 💡 Storyteller info (do not read out loud)

-   The crew is being dumped on a space-ship in disrepair.
-   The players need to familiarize themselves with the ship, fix the systems and start exploring the Void.
-   This scene will teach them how to fix, power and man the ship's systems, chart the nearby points of interest, and start the spin engine to jump away.
-   During this, you need to foreshadow the main antagonist the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-horror" /> by mentioning strange things happening throughout the ship and something unseen trying to get inside. E.g. frost gathering on windows, things moving on its own, gravity anomalies, indistinct whispers, ship hull creaking.
-   When the ship is all but ready to jump away, the <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="void-horror" /> appears in all its might and attacks the ship. It must be clear the Shadow will destroy them easily, if they stay.

## Introduction

_They called it mercy._

_Whatever you did to deserve this, the Empire decided to exile you. You were loaded onto a rusting corvette and pushed toward the border of what the star charts call the **Hungry Void**._

_Once, the Void had been the Empire’s frontier—rich in minerals, strange worlds, and relics of civilizations older than history. Too many ships vanished. Colonies died without reason. So eventually, the Empire withdrew, sealed the gates, and left the region and its survivors to their own devices. <GameCard type="ActorCard" slug="smugglers" />, pirates, and desperate souls like you now call this sorry place their own._

_An officer unceremoniously shoves you into a small shuttle. *"Cross back into the Empire space, and you’ll be shot on sight." He spits out before slamming the shuttle door shut.* The autopilot engages and your shuttle silently exits out of the huge docking bay doors._

🎭 **This is the first time you see one another. Tell us what the others see, when they look at you.**

_You reach a small retired military vessel and park in its docking bay. Once a proud corvette, now a gutted relic with half its systems stripped, the other half broken, its hull held together by sheer faith. Its original name has been scraped off._
_A blink of light illuminates the dark interior as the Imperial transport jumps away, leaving you adrift at the edge of nothing._

As you gather on the bridge, you take stock of the ship:

-   *The reactor* is running on minimal power, supplying only enough power to feed the life-support.
-   *Life-support* is operational, but some rooms are unpressurized.
-   *The engines* are not responding and the spin drive needs to be charged before you can jump away.
-   All but one of the *weapon systems* were uninstalled. A single laser array is left and malfunctioning.
-   There is a *missile bay*, but there are no missiles.
-   The *crew quarters* has space for 8 people.
-   There should be some more rooms, but the corridor is sealed off.
-   Sensors need to be calibrated, before you can survey nearby points of interest.
-   There is a *cargo bay* with a single box of food rations.

**It’s time to make this wreck fly**

🎭 **Who wants to start and tell me what you do?**

### 📅 When the crew starts charging the Spin Drive

_The ship trembles as the Spin Drive begins to hum, a deep metallic vibration that crawls through the floor and into your bones. The lights flicker once, twice — then turn into a dim red pulse. You notice the space outside the cockpit window begins to… twist?! It doesn’t bend or move; it folds on top of itself, like a melted sheet of glass, straining… until it snaps._

_A rift tears open to the Void. A darkness spills through, blacker than the space around it, tiny fake star-like lights glitter as they move unnaturally over the darkness. Twisting tendrils of shadow creep out, stretching larger than the ship itself forming into a terrifying entity._

_Every screen flares with static. Warning glyphs flicker and die. Metal groans in agony as the ship’s systems begin to fail — not destroyed, but drained. The heat leaves the air. Frost creeps across consoles and bulkheads in seconds. You see your breath. Then, from somewhere deep in the hull, the sound of rending steel scratches your ear drums._

_The shadow tendrils are inside. They slither through cracks, and seams. Every surface they touch loses color, temperature, and light._

_The hum of the Spin Drive turns frantic, building toward an unstable crescendo. If it fails now, you are stranded._

### Systems overview

-   Ship systems can take as much damage as is their energy consumption or output.
-   Each damage reduces the available energy consumption or output by 1PU.
-   Damage can be repaired by player action, 1 damage repaired per Effect.

-   *Reactor* — Provides power, determines how many ship systems can be active at the same time. Manning reactor can produce extra energy.
-   *Engines* — Provide thrust to move around and maneuver during fights. Manning engines can provide extra speed.
-   *Spin drive* — Jumps the ship to other systems. Manning spin drive can reduce spin-up time.
-   *Weapon systems* — Fires weapons. Manning this increases damage or fire rate.
-   *Missile bay* — Launches missiles. Manning this is required to load and target missiles.
-   *Sensors* — Give information about space around you, find other ships and their systems, plot course to points of interest. Manning sensors gives more information.
-   *Life support* — Controls temperature and air pressure. Manning this can pressurize or depressurize rooms.
-   *Cockpit* — Manuevers the ship. Manning cockpit can provide evasion.
